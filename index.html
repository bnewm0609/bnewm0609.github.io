About Me

Hello! I'm Ben Newman, a current junior at Stanford studying Computer Science. My interests include in computer and cognitive science, linguistics, and education.

This summer I am working at Stanford's Center for the Study of Language and Information (CSLI) on a project involving using context to evaluate Natural Language Generation systems with Prof. Christopher Potts.

My interests in communication and learning have brought me to research at the intersection of natural language processing and cognitive science. I'm especially interested in the role of structure in learning, the status of language generation models as communicative agents, and the use of computer sicence to improve interpersonal communication. I've worked on projects on language learning in spatial scenarios, transliterating English names into Chinese, and modeling common ground information with a differentiable neural computer. As an intern with Supplyhouse.com, I used NLP techniques to analyze customer service interactions. At Stanford Splash I co-teach courses in Introductory Linguistics and Computing Fundamentals, and I am involved with the Stanford NLP group.

Currently, I am also a manager at Stanford's Challah for Hunger organization, where we are "baking" a difference by baking and selling bread to fight food insecurity on-campus and worldwide.

I am also a fan robotics: I have worked on avionics for the Stanford Student Space Initiative's (SSI) world-record setting High Altitude Ballons team. I am also an alumnus and mentor of FIRST FRC Team 2638. Go Rebels!

You can find a link to my resume below as well as some of my projects.
